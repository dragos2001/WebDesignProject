Christmas

Cluj Christmas Market

On Christmas time in Cluj the square is nicely decorated and its centrepiece is an enormous Christmas tree with lights and presents underneath. The entrances all have arches decorated with gold lights. There is a big top like frame of lights in front of the main arena. The entire site looks pretty and especially brightened up when there is an act on the main stage. Each of the stalls has pleasant touches on too to reflect the theme of their wares.


The main ride in the Christmas fair is the Feris Wheel, which overlooks the entire site. This has fantastic views and is lit up with bright lights too. The other significant ride is the carrousel, which is more of a children’s ride. The main stage in the square is pretty cool and has a fantastic lighting rig. A variety of performances will use the stage over the 6 week fair. This includes some iconic Romanian musicians, like Voltaj and Directia 5. These two were great on the opening weekend and despite the rain, the square had a great vibe.


There are a variety of stalls around the square selling all sorts of things. Many sell food to consume when at the festivities, including fast food, cakes and Kurtos. You can also purchase a variety of Christmas gifts, including some handmade items from Transylvania. Also, many food producers use the market to sell their wares. The charcuterie and cheese stalls are particularly interesting and sometimes have free samples.

Music

Untold Festival

Untold Festival is the largest electronic music festival held in Romania, taking place in Cluj-Napoca at the Cluj Arena. It is held annually and has cemented itself as one of Europe’s premiere dance music events thanks to its groundbreaking stage designs and elite lineup. It has been designated Best Major Festival in the European Festival Awards 2015.


Guests come from a vast range of European countries, as well as Asia and North America to witness the shows of some of the world’s music stars like Armin van Buuren, Tiësto, David Guetta, Black Eyed Peas, Robbie Williams, The Prodigy, Boris Brejcha and Parov Stellar.


UNTOLD 2023 will take place in the first week of August, starting on 3th and ending on the 6th. Over 200,000 music fans are expected to gather in Cluj-Napoca for this event. Electric Castle

Electric Castle is an annual music festival held on the Transylvanian domain of the Bánffy Castle, near Cluj-Napoca in Romania, and combines music, technology and alternative arts. It features many genres of music including rock, indie, hip hop, electronic, techno, and drum and bass as well as art installations.


In the seventh edition, the festival has received the award for the Best Medium Sized Festival at the European Festival Awards, where Electric Castle has been nominated since its existence. On its past editions, the festival had confirmed names such as Florence + the Machine, Thirty Seconds to Mars, The Prodigy, Nils Frahm, Skrillex, Bring me the Horizon, Deadmau5, alt-J, Franz Ferdinand, Fatboy Slim, Sigur Ros, Bastille, Rudimental, Thievery Corporation, Die Antwoord, Twenty One Pilots, Gorillaz and many other artists that performed on one of its ten stages.


Last year's lineup consisted of Twenty one pilots, Gorillaz, Deftones, Disclosure, Peggy Gou, Purple Disco Machine, Mogwai, Moderat and lots of other big artists. If you are looking forward to the festival you can click this link to the official festival site so you get all the latest information.

Jazz in the Park

Just as the summer officially turns to autumn, between September 1st and 4th, another music festival lands in the city – or in the park, for that matter. The Jazz festival in the National Ethnographic Park “Romulus Vuia” will take place in each afternoon and evening from Thursday to Sunday.


There will be dozens of jazz names performing, and this will be the 10th edition. It is the most important jazz festival in Romania, and in 2019 it was named Europe’s best small festival.


The park is just outside the city, in the middle of the nature, and fans can also visit a traditional open air museum.

Movies

Transilvania International Film Festival (TIFF)

The Transilvania International Film Festival (TIFF) is the first international feature film festival in Romania, which is held annually in the historic capital of Transylvania, Cluj-Napoca.


It was founded in 2002 by the Romanian Film Promotion and has since grown rapidly to become the most important film-related event in Romania. It is a member of the Alliance of Central and Eastern European Film Festivals and it is supported by the Creative Europe – MEDIA Programme. In February 2011, TIFF has been accredited by the FIAPF as a "competitive festival specialised on first and second feature films". Indiewire listed it as one of the world's top 50 leading film festivals.


The festival takes place in 20 locations around the city, including outdoor and unconventional places. Transilvania IFF's main goal is the promotion of cinematic art by presenting some of the most innovative and spectacular films of the moment that feature both originality and independence of expression, that reflect unusual cinematic language forms or focus on current trends in youth culture. The competition is dedicated to first and second feature films.
