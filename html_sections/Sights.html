Top attractions in Cluj - Napoca

Cetatiua Park

Cetățuia is a fortification built during the Habsburg period at the top of a hill sharing the same name in Cluj-Napoca , in the Gruia district . The citadel was built between 1715 and 1735 and was surrounded by bastions, according to the plans of the military architect Giovanni Morando Visconti. The access gates from the west and south no longer exist, only those from the south-east and north remain standing. Above the gates and within the enclosure were various buildings that housed administrative quarters, a garrison, and a weapons depot.


The fortification was built with the main purpose of ensuring control over the city, after the passage of Transylvania under Austrian rule, but it was also used many times as a prison, especially during the Revolution of 1848.
Little remains of the old citadel: part of the walls, four buildings, and the redoubt was transformed into the Parachutist Tower. The whole complex was deeply affected by the landslides in the area.


The Belvedere Hotel was built on the hill of the hill in the 1970s, and in 1995 a huge iron cross was erected ( Crucea de pe Cetătuie din Cluj-Napoca ), in place of the one destroyed by the communists in the 1950s . The original cross was erected in memory of those imprisoned here.

Alex Borza Botanical Garden

Alexandru Borza Cluj-Napoca University Botanic Garden, is a botanical garden located in the south part of Cluj. It was founded in 1872 by Hungarian linguist Sámuel Brassai. In addition to its role as a tourist destination, the garden also serves as a teaching and research center as part of the Babeș-Bolyai University. In 2010, the Romanian Ministry of Culture and National Patrimony categorized it as a historical monument.


The garden is over 14 hectares in area, with over 10,000 plants found throughout the world. It is divided into ornamental, phytogeographic (geobotanical), systematic (taxonomical), economic, and medicinal sections. Romanian flora and vegetation are represented by plants from the Transylvanian plains, the Carpathian Mountains, Banat, etc.


Among the Botanical Garden's interesting attractions are the Japanese Garden (a garden in Japanese style, with a brook and a Japanese-style house), the Roman Garden with archeological remains from the Roman colony of Napoca, among them a statue of Ceres, goddess of cereals and bread, alongside cultivated plants that dominate contemporary Romanian agriculture. Central Park "Simion Bărnutiu"

The "Simion Bărnutiu" Central Park, colloquially the Great Park, in Hungarian Sétatér, with an age of over 180 years, is one of the main green spaces in the central area of ​​Cluj-Napoca. The park is bounded by the Cluj Arena to the west, the Hungarian State Theater to the east, the Someșul Mic river to the north, and Iuliu Hossu Street(formerly Pavlov) to the south. It is listed on the list of historical monuments in Cluj county, developed by the Romanian Ministry of Culture and National Heritage in 2010.


Since the beginning of the 19th century, the location of the current park has become a favorite meeting place for the city's middle-class residents. The first attempts to transform the place into a public park, however, did not enjoy the material and moral support of the noble families of Cluj, who owned large ornamental gardens


In the park are the statues of personalities, such as Antonin Ciolan , George Coșbuc , Liviu Rebreanu , Octavian Goga , Sigismund Toduță , erected after 1960, and more recently, the Romanian composer, baritone and opera director Nicolae Bretan .

Julius Park

Julius Park offers the residents of Cluj a green space designed according to modern standards, similar to those in large European cities. It is an oasis of relaxation in the city, for all visitors to enjoy the most pleasant ways of recreation and disconnection in nature: 10,000 m2 of lawn for heavy traffic, pedestrian walkway suspended above the lake, cycling and running track, paths promenade and relaxation spaces equipped with benches.


Iulius Parc covers an area of ​​three hectares and is an investment of over two million euros by the Iulius Group, made for the Cluj community. The redevelopment works involved the regeneration of the vegetation by planting over 18,000 trees and shrubs of various sizes and approximately 10,000 square meters of lawn especially for traffic.

Art Museum of Cluj-Napoca

The Museum of Cluj-Napoca or National Art Museum, is an art museum housed in an important eighteenth-century Baroque building, the Cluj-Napoca Bánffy Palace, designed by German architect Johann Eberhard Blaumann. The museum possesses a very valuable collection of Romanian and European art: paintings, graphics and decorative art ranging from the Fifteenth Century to the Twentieth.


With a collection of over 12,000 paintings, sculptures, graphic arts and decorative pieces, the museum is considered one of the most important museums in Romania


The artists are represented in the gallery by the most important of their works, having in view both the revealing of the characteristic aspects of the multi-culture specificity in Transylvania and suggesting of the complex relations between the art in Romania and the main tendences and streams of the European one. By including fully consacrated values into the synthesis of national post-war art, created in Transylvania and especially in Cluj, the museum remains receptive also to the creations of more recent date.

St. Michael's Church and Matthias Corvinus Monument

St. Michael's Church (Romanian: Biserica Sfântul Mihail, Hungarian: Szent Mihály-templom, German: Michaelskirche) is a Gothic-style Roman Catholic church in Cluj-Napoca. It is the second largest church (after the Biserica Neagră of Brașov) in the geographical region of Transylvania, Romania. The nave is 50 meters long and 24 meters wide, the apse is 20×10 m. The tower with its height of 76 meter (80 meter including the cross) is the highest one in Transylvania.


In front of the Church there is The Matthias Corvinus Monument. This classified historic monument, conceived by János Fadrusz and opened in 1902, represents Matthias Corvinus, who was King of Hungary and Croatia from 1458 to 1490. After conducting several military campaigns, he was elected King of Bohemia in 1469 and adopted the title Duke of Austria in 1487.


The monument is listed in the National Register of Historic Monuments in Romania. After casting the sculpture in bronze in the ore foundry, another small plaster model was made and sent to the World Exhibition in Paris. The plaster sample has been awarded the Grand Prix, the highest award at the World Expo.
