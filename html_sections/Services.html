Public Transportation

The public transport system in Cluj-Napoca is equiped with a network of trolleys, buses and trams from which 50% are electrical. They are whizzing around the city from morning to night. The buses cover the entire city, with almost everything accessible via one of the three options. Tickets cost 3 RON per trip and can be purchased from ticket machines near the bus station or from the vehicle .

Safety

Cluj-Napoca is a very safe city. The index of crime is very low here. Cluj-Napoca has 7 Municipal Police Stations for each main neighborhood and 3 firefighting Stations. In case of emergency contact 112.

Town Hall

The Cluj-Napoca City Hall, located at 3 Moților Street, is the seat of government for Cluj-Napoca, Romania. Built at the end of the 19th century after the plans of architect Ignác Alpár, it features a Viennese baroque facade with a corner clock tower.

Airport

Avram Iancu Cluj International Airport is an airport serving the city of Cluj-Napoca, Romania. Initially known as Someșeni Airport, it is located 9 km (5.6 mi) east of the city centre, in the Someșeni area, which is now within the Cluj-Napoca city limits. The airport is named in honour of Romanian revolutionary Avram Iancu.

Shopping

Cluj-Napoca has 3 large commercial shopping centers including Iulius Mall, Vivo! and Platinia Shopping Center, each of them containg various shops and a supermarket.

Working

Cluj-Napoca is an important center in IT in Eastern Europe being considered the 'Silicon Valey' of Eastern Europe. Also it has a general need of personenl in both private and public domains, in industries like IT, medicine, economy, workers and state department.

Education

Cluj Napoca is the second most important university center in Romania with half of the population of Cluj being students. It has 5 universities covering fields such as natural sciences, mathematics, engineering, agricultural sciences, medicine, etc.
